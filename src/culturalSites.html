The Hamar People

Location South Omo, South Nations Nationalities and Peoples, Ethiopia
Coordinates 5°30'N 36°30'E
Climate arid

The Hamar are known for their unique custom of "bull jumping," which initiates a boy into manhood. First, female relatives dance and invite whipping from men who have recently been initiated; this shows their support of the initiate, and their scars give them a say on who they marry.


The popular traditional evangadi dance of the hamar people:

Evangadi is a night time dance ceremony. Usually it happens after harvesting their crops which, represent their happiness. During night they can be able to see the full moon. This show's peace, love and unity. And also it is the time that all young men and women come together for relationship.
